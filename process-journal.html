Friendbot Process Journal


"Map out the interactions that will be required to assist people in interacting with autonomous vehicles in future cities"

EMPATHISE

After our group decided on a problem space of international airports, we seperately conducted our own research and different research methods to find true problem areas that arise during a passangers visit to an international airport.

How?

I went onsite to The Sydney International Airport and conducted nine semi-structured interviews and three think aloud sessions. After conducting my part of the primary research, I discovered four main problem areas: wayfinding, checking in, baggage and post check in experience

User Journey Map

Learning about our users

After conducting my initial interviews and think aloud sessions, I created personas and from those personas I generated an user journey map outlining the average journey a user would partake throughout their experience to an airport.
As you can see from the diagram pictured, arriving at the airport is a high, positive point for the users, as they are excited for their impending journey. However, as they start the process of checking in, the user becomes flustered and anxious. The users experience becomes even more negative when they have to ask for staff asistance. The overall experience only starts to become positive again when they are getting ready to board.

IDEATE

Concept #1 - Lug My Luggage

Lug my Luggage is a vehicle that simplifies and autonomises the check-in process, particularly in the aspect of baggage. This baggage system secures and transports users’ luggage from check-in, directly to the plane to ensure safety and security with baggage handling. This concept addresses the concern that the user had of security. Use the carousel below to view the storyboard, screen sketches and a paper prototype.

Previous Next

Unfortunetly, this concept had a lot of downfalls. After consulting with our tutor and testing this with a number of users, we discovered that whilst the aim was to improve the security concerns of the issue, it actually caused more problems. Passangers had trust issues not knowing where their belongings or checked baggage was going and if it was going to the correct location. The face scanning concept could also cause security issues. Another issue we had was the autonomous aspect of the design. It wasnt the main feature, therefore didnt adhere to the brief.

Concept #2 - Friendbot

Friendbot is an autonomous robot that greets travellers upon entering the airport and guides them through the airport as well as provides convenient services including food and duty free delivery. This concept addresses the concern that the user had of wayfinding, notifications and security. We created two versions of this design, as initially we were going to go further with this design. After consulting with out tutor, our group decided that we wanted to merge the two concepts into one to create a more elevated idea. Decisions that were improved included:

  • No need for the "motherbot" anymore
  • Eliminated motion sensor
  • Eliminated face scanning as it posed security issues. boarding pass was suggested instead
  • Eliminated voice command, as the noise of the airport would conflict with the voice of the user
  • Eliminated the option to pick if travelling solo or in a group as our user testing proved that we didnt need this feature.
  • Changed the search process for additional activities to make the user flow more organic
  • Eliminated cash and paypal as paying by card was the only feasible option
  • Added a tracking buzzer that allows Friendbot to locate the user when the bot collects shopping and food.
  • Changed the process of payment so the user pays before the food was delivered to stop theft and wastage

Concept #3 - NaviGATE

naviGATE is an autonomous pod that travels from the security exit to all ends of the airport to deliver passengers to their gates in a safe and timely fashion. This concept addresses the concern that the user had of wayfinding and notifications. Use the two carousels below to view the storyboards, screen sketches and a paper prototypes of both concepts. Please note, these carousels are automatic and not clickable, I couldnt get the click to work for some reason.

Our group really liked this concept, however after conducting very basic initial user testing and consulting with our tutor we decided to make the following changes:

  • Make the pods personalised, so one pod per travel group.
  • Make the pods smaller so they can navigate the airport quicker.
  • Travellers only have the option to travel to their gate unless there is a delay. This would improve time optimisation.



User Testing Paper Prototypes

After considerations, we decided to go ahead with two out of our three concepts. We chose Friendbot and naviGATE. We user tested both of the paper prototypes with a variety of methods. We utalised think aloud, observations and semi-structured interviews to fully gauge our users response. In total we tested 16 users. We chose users who have travelled overseas often and have been to both foreign and local airports.

naviGATE Results
  • Users would have preferred to have the option to stop by other locations on the way to their gate. The wanted more freedom in their destinations.
  • Users would have preferred to have all the maps present on the one screen so that they can see all their options at once.
  • Would like to have more details about the options and destinations when there is a delay. Want this information to be displayed once at a time.
  • Would like to have information about HOW to perform a task as well as WHY you are performing that task.
Friendbot Results
  • Users would like some more details on the maps like an estimated time of arrival so they can plan out their time and be more informed.
  • Users would like to be able to have the freedom of taking a slightly different path while walking to their gate. They want to be able to visit other stores or places.
  • Users would like to have a “MENU” button so that they can easily navigate back to the main service menu where ever they are.
  • Restrictions on being able to order services or food if they do not have enough time for it to arrive before boarding the plane.

PROTOTYPE



Based off our user testing results, we conducted heuristic evaluation and decision matrices to decide that our final concept will be Friendbot.
We took all the feedback from our testers and created interactive wireframes. Here is a video walkthrough of our wireframes.

User Testing Wireframes

Using Sketch and invision, we created interactive wireframes so that we could do another round of user testing before we make our final interactive prototype. Because our concept has a lot of screens and a lot of different situations that could happen, we decided to split our testing into two different scenarios, as this made it easier to get more input from our users. The first scenario had the user get to their gate by going through the check-in process and then they had to order food. The second scenario involved the user watching a movie on the robot.

Friendbot Results
  • Would like delays and notification alerts to pop up on the screen rather than be discreetly received because it is important information.
  • Would like a voice command option to end the service rather than having to find a button that ends it. This could be clearer and more effective.
  • Would like to see some additional information regarding the bot leaving and returning at security exit.
  • Would like to see a “MORE INFO” button for the tracking device information so they could learn more about the function.


Final Product

After user testing our wireframes, we created an initial round of interactive mockups on Framer X. We had alot of issues on Framer X that resulted in our process becoming slower. Framer X was very slow and unresponsive once our number of screens increased dramatically. We also had trouble sharing our screens so we could user test and even tually submit. For the user testing we had to just use my laptop. We really wanted to use an Ipad so the user would get a feel for the screen size, but as mentioned, the sharing capabilites on Framer X were poor. We user tested the screens on users from a wide age group, all frequent travellers. Because we did alot of iteration and ideation in our paper prototyping stage, the issues we came across when testing this time were few and far between. The main issues we had included:

  • The alert for gate and boarding time changes wasnt effective. We changed this so the notification pops up automatically.
  • There wasn't a back button. Users wanted a way to go back but they didnt really like the idea of a back button. We implemented a swipe feature where the user swipes to go bsck a screen just like on a smartphone. We also included a tutorial on how to do this on the very first screen.
